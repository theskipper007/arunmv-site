<Tldr hint="for the impatient">

Long runs and long debugging sessions reward the same thing: a **steady pace** and the discipline not to sprint at the first sign of trouble.

</Tldr>

I picked up distance running a few years ago, and I keep noticing how much it mirrors the way I
work through hard bugs.

## Don't sprint the first mile

The temptation in both is to go hard immediately — change five things at once, or set off too
fast. <mark>You pay for it later.</mark> A sustainable pace gets you further.

## Trust the process

There's a stretch in every long run where you can't see progress and want to quit. Debugging has
the same valley. The trick is the same: keep moving methodically, and trust that the finish is
closer than it feels.
